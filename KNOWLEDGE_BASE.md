## üè´ Scout Sections Overview

### **Sungura Scouts** (Ages 6-10)
- **Stages**: Nyota I, II, III
- **Key Badges**: Animal Husbandry, Artist, Athlete, Conservation, Drug Abuse Prevention, First Aider, Musician, Photographer, etc.
- **Focus**: Foundation skills, basic leadership, character development

### **Chipukizi Scouts** (Ages 10-14)
- **Stages**: Zizi, Shina, Tawi
- **Key Badges**: Air Apprentice, Backwoodsman, Camper, Canoeist, Citizenship, Communicator, Cook, Explorer, Fire Fighter, etc.
- **Focus**: Intermediate skills, leadership development, social responsibility

### **Mwamba Scouts** (Ages 14-18)
- **Stages**: Mwanzo, Mwangaza, Kilele
- **Special Badges**: Bushman's Thong, Airman's Badge, Seaman's Badge, Simba Badge
- **Key Badges**: Air Mechanic, Archaeologist, Artist, Astronomer, Boatswain, Electrician, Explorer, Forester, Hiker, Mountaineer, etc.
- **Focus**: Advanced skills, outdoor mastery, specialized expertise

### **Jasiri Scouts** (Ages 18+)
- **Focus**: Adult leadership and mentoring
- **Key Awards**: Chief Scout Award (CSA), Chief Commissioner's Challenge Badge, Project Badge
- **Key Badges**: Mountain Rescue, Mwalimu (Education), Instructor, Lifesaver, etc.

## üë• Founder & History

### **Lord Baden-Powell (Robert Stephen Smith)**
- **Born**: February 22, 1857 (England)
- **Career**: British Army officer (India, South Africa)
- **Key Moment**: Siege of Mafeking - resourcefulness under siege
- **Publications**:
  - "Aids to Scouting" (1899)
  - "Scouting For Boys" (1908)
- **Connection to Kenya**:
  - First visit: 1906
  - Settled: 1938
  - Home: "PAXTU" (Nyeri)
  - Died: January 8, 1941 (buried in Nyeri)

### **Scouting Origins**
- First experimental camp: 1907, Brownsea Island, England
- Inspired by boys organizing themselves into "Scouts"
- Adapted to cultures worldwide while maintaining core values
